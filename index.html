Key findings & policy recommendations — NCRB Crime in India 2024
1. Total crime rate — AP 24.6% below All India national averageAP's combined rate of 316.1/lakh is 24.6% below All India 418.9. Total crimes have declined 13.6% from 1,95,284 (2022) to 1,68,788 (2024). AP ranks 18/28 states on total crime rate (17 states better, 10 states worse).
2. Chargesheeting rate — AP's strongest metric: Rank 3/28 nationallyAP's overall CSR (90.7%) is 3rd nationally — only Kerala (97.7%) and Gujarat (97.2%) higher. Women crime CSR ranks 2nd (94.4%). Children crime CSR ranks 6th (88.5%, +27.1 pts above national). Only cybercrime CSR (28.6%) falls below national (31.9%).
3. IPC/BNS and SLL rates — both below national averageIPC/BNS rate (217.4) is 13.8% below All India (252.3). SLL rate (98.7) is 40.7% below All India (166.6). AP ranks 18/28 on both IPC and SLL rates individually, with 17 states having lower rates in each category.
4. Women crime rate — 15.6% above national, with five concerning sub-categoriesOverall rate 74.7 vs national 64.6. Sub-category concerns: Insult to Modesty (7.9× national), Stalking (2.6×), Cruelty by Husband (1.95×), Voyeurism (1.7×), Foeticide (slightly above). However strong performance on Rape (60% lower), K&A of Women (90% lower), Dowry Deaths (62% lower), POCSO (32% lower). The 21.8% decline in total women crimes from 2022 is a significant positive.
5. Children crime — AP's best performing major category: Rank 4/28Children crime rate (17.4/lakh child pop) is 58.9% below national (42.3). Only Nagaland, Manipur and Jharkhand have lower rates. CSR of 88.5% is +27.1 pts above national. Cases declining from 3,308 (2022) to 2,633 (2024) — ↓20.4%.
6. Senior citizens crime — 37.6% above national, increasing trendRate (43.2/lakh elderly) exceeds national (31.4). Cases: 2,114 (2022) → 1,837 (2023) → 2,090 (2024). While CSR is strong (86.4% vs national 74.4%), the incidence rate needs attention. Recommend: dedicated Senior Citizen Protection Cell and priority FIR registration for elderly.
7. SC crimes — 22% below national, strong 3-year declining trendRate (21.6/lakh SC pop) is 22% below national (27.7). Cases declining consistently: 2,315 → 2,027 → 1,823 (↓21.3%). States like West Bengal (0.5), Punjab (1.9), Chhattisgarh (8.2) demonstrate significantly lower rates — aspirational benchmarks.
8. Night Burglary — AP rate (6.1) slightly below national (6.3), total burglary on parTotal burglary rate (7.6) is essentially equal to national (7.7). Night burglary AP (3,231 cases, rate 6.1) vs national (89,062 cases, rate 6.3) — AP is slightly better. Day burglary AP (830, rate 1.6) is marginally above national (rate 1.3).
9. Missing persons — AP traces 70.0% vs national 54.3%; girls tracing 90.5%AP's missing persons tracing — especially children (87.4% vs national 67.2%) and adult females (83.4% vs 56.1%) — is significantly above national averages. This reflects effective police coordination and AHTU networks.
10. Cybercrime — low rate (35.6% below national) but low CSR (28.6%) signals gapsTelangana (71.1 rate) and Karnataka (32.2 rate) dominate nationally, partly due to stronger dedicated cybercrime infrastructure. AP should invest in dedicated cybercrime police stations and digital forensics to improve detection and investigation quality.
11. Benchmarking targets for AP 2025–26(a) Women crime: reduce Cruelty by Husband towards Gujarat levels (19.3 rate) through DV cells; (b) Senior citizens: adopt Jharkhand model (rate 1.3 — lowest); (c) SC crimes: target West Bengal (0.5) and Punjab (1.9) as aspirational; (d) Cybercrime CSR: investment in forensic labs to achieve 50%+ CSR; (e) Children crime: sustain current trajectory — already rank 4 nationally.
