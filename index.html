Key findings & policy recommendations — NCRB Crime in India 2024
1. Total crime rate — AP 24.6% below national averageAP's combined IPC+SLL rate of 316.1 per lakh is 24.6% below the All India national average of 418.9, reflecting an overall positive crime control environment. Total cognizable crimes have consistently declined from 1,95,284 (2022) to 1,68,788 (2024) — a 13.6% reduction over two years.
2. IPC/BNS rate — AP (217.4) is 13.8% BELOW national average (252.3)AP's IPC/BNS crime rate of 217.4 per lakh is below the All India national average of 252.3, placing AP favourably on the primary crime metric. AP ranks approximately 17th out of 28 states — near the median but below the national average. High-IPC states such as Kerala (513.0), Telangana (497.7), Odisha (366.9), Haryana (368.5), MP (321.6), and Maharashtra (300.0) perform significantly worse. The focus should be on closing the gap with states like Karnataka (203.3), Bihar (184.8) and UP (180.2) to move into the top-10.
3. Chargesheeting rates — AP's consistent strength across all categoriesAP's CSR of 90.7% is 10.4 percentage points above national (80.3%). More strikingly, AP outperforms nationally in every crime category: Crime against Women (94.4% vs 77.2%), Crime against Children (88.5% vs 61.4%), Kidnapping & Abduction (67.8% vs 30.9%), Senior Citizens (86.4% vs 74.4%), Economic Offences (70.6% vs 53.3%). Only Cybercrime (28.6% vs 31.9%) is marginally below national.
4. Offences against women — mixed picture requiring urgent sub-category actionAP's total women & child offences rate (41.8 per lakh female pop) is 66% above national (25.2). Cruelty by Husband (34.3 vs national 17.6 — 1.95× higher), Stalking (3.7 vs 1.4 — 2.64×), Insult to Modesty (11.1 vs 1.4 — 7.9×) are major concerns. However, AP excels on Rape (60% lower), K&A of Women (90% lower), Dowry Deaths (62% lower) and POCSO (32% lower). A targeted cruelty by husband and stalking enforcement programme is needed.
5. Road accident deaths — above national average; highway safety priority neededAP's road accident death rate (14.8 per lakh) exceeds the national average (12.1 per lakh). Other road accidents (6,982 cases, rate 13.1 vs national 7.8) are the dominant driver. This warrants focused highway patrol, speed enforcement, and black-spot remediation — particularly on National Highways 16, 65, and 44.
6. Crimes against children — AP is 58.9% below national rateAP's crime against children rate (17.4 per lakh child population) is among the lowest nationally, compared to national average of 42.3. POCSO cases (1,778 at rate 6.7 vs national 9.9) also reflect better child safety. Children CSR of 88.5% vs national 61.4% shows strong police follow-through.
7. SC crimes — AP 22% below national; consistent improvement over 3 yearsAP's SC crimes (PoA Act) rate of 21.6 per lakh SC population is 22% below the national average of 27.7. The trend (2,315→2,027→1,823) shows consistent improvement. However, states like Assam (0.1), West Bengal (0.4), Punjab (1.3) and Jharkhand (4.4) demonstrate significantly lower rates, indicating room for major improvement through social reform and targeted policing.
8. Senior citizen crimes — 53.8% above national; needs dedicated strategyAP's senior citizen crime rate (48.3 per lakh elderly population) significantly exceeds the national average (31.4). While CSR is strong (86.4% vs national 74.4%), the incidence rate is a concern. A dedicated Senior Citizen Protection Cell, property fraud awareness, and priority FIR registration for elderly complainants are recommended.
9. Missing persons — AP traces 70.0% vs national 54.3%; children tracing at 87.4%AP's missing persons tracing performance — particularly for children (87.4% vs national 67.2%) and girls specifically (90.5% vs national 67.3%) — reflects effective police responsiveness. Adult female tracing (83.4%) is also well above the national average (56.1%). These systems should be institutionalised as a national model.
10. Cybercrime — low rate but investigation quality needs investmentAP's cybercrime rate (4.7 vs national 7.3) appears positive, but CSR of 28.6% (below national 31.9%) signals investigation gaps. Invest in dedicated cyber police stations, digital forensics labs, and trained investigators. States like Karnataka demonstrate that stronger detection infrastructure leads to better case disposal outcomes.
11. Benchmarking targets for AP 2025–26(a) IPC/BNS rate: target Telangana-level trajectory; (b) Cruelty by Husband: target Karnataka (lower incidence + strong enforcement); (c) SC crimes: target Jharkhand (rate 4.4) and Punjab (rate 1.3) as aspirational models; (d) Senior Citizen crimes: adopt dedicated task forces similar to Tamil Nadu; (e) Road accident deaths: target Hit & Run reduction (AP 1.3 already better than national 3.8 — leverage this strength).

Source: Crime in India 2024 (Volumes I, II & III) — National Crime Records Bureau (NCRB), Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India.
