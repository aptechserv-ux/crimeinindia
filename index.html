Key findings, analytical conclusions & policy recommendations — NCRB 2024
1. Overall burden — AP significantly below national averageAP's IPC/BNS crime rate (98.7) is 41.7% below the national average of 169.4. Combined IPC+SLL rate (316.1) is 22.3% below national (406.8). AP ranks 16th out of 28 states on IPC/BNS rate — better than the national median.
2. Chargesheeting rate — AP's standout strength (90.7% vs 80.3%)AP's chargesheeting rate of 90.7% exceeds the national average of 80.3% by 10.4 percentage points, placing AP among the top 5 states nationally. This demonstrates strong police investigation discipline and prosecution follow-through across all crime categories.
3. Crimes against women — mixed picture with critical concernsAP excels on rape (1.7 vs national 4.3), murder (1.7 vs 2.07), attempt to murder (2.9 vs 4.2), dowry deaths (72 cases), and stalking (0.3 vs 0.8). However, cruelty by husband (41.8 vs national 12.2 — 3.4x higher), voyeurism (3.7 vs 1.5), disrobing (11.1 vs 1.3 — 8.5x higher), insult to modesty (34.3 vs 17.3 — 2x), and acid attacks (5.8 vs 1.2 — 5x) are major areas of concern requiring urgent attention.
4. Acid attacks — most alarming sub-category (Rate 5.8 vs national 1.2)AP's acid attack rate of 5.8 per lakh (3,111 cases) is approximately 5 times the national average of 1.2 (national cases: 16,099). This is the single most alarming finding in the 2024 data and demands a dedicated investigation, fast-track prosecution under S.326A/B BNS, and victim support mechanism.
5. Cruelty by husband/relatives — 3.4x national average (Rate 41.8 vs 12.2)AP registered 17,480 cruelty by husband cases (S.85-86 BNS/498A IPC) against a national state average rate of 12.2. This could reflect both better reporting (positive) and genuine domestic violence prevalence. Domestic violence cells, shelter homes, and community awareness programmes need scaling up.
6. BNS transition surge — 73.2% jump in IPC cases needs contextual understandingIPC/BNS cases jumped from 30,426 (2023) to 52,712 (2024). A significant portion is attributable to Wrongful Restraint (S.126-127 BNS — 36,093 cases at 67.6 per lakh), a category newly made cognizable under BNS. This artificially inflates the rate and must be filtered when assessing genuine crime trends.
7. Motor Vehicles Act — above national (87.5 vs 71.2) but reflects enforcement rigourAP's MV Act rate (87.5 per lakh) exceeds the national average (71.2) with 46,726 cases in 2024. This partly reflects aggressive traffic enforcement, which is a positive indicator of road safety discipline. However, road accident deaths (7,915 cases under causing death by negligence at rate 14.8) warrant continued highway safety measures.
8. SC atrocities — below national but room for major improvementAP's SC atrocity rate (20.9 per lakh SC population, 1,765 cases) is below the national average of 24.7 but far higher than model states: West Bengal (0.4), Punjab (1.3), Jharkhand (4.4), and Himachal Pradesh (3.0). Strengthening SC/ST Courts, sensitisation training for police, and community-level monitoring are needed.
9. Cybercrime — low rate but very low chargesheeting rate (28.6%)AP's cybercrime rate of 4.7 per lakh (2,528 cases) is below the national average of 7.3, but the chargesheeting rate of only 28.6% (vs 31.9% nationally) is very low. Investment in dedicated cyber police stations, digital forensics labs, and trained cybercrime investigators is essential. Karnataka's 21,437 registrations reveal AP may have significant under-detection.
10. Human trafficking — AP excels (159 cases, 100% CSR)AP registered 159 human trafficking cases in 2024 with a remarkable 100% chargesheeting rate, indicating a well-functioning Anti-Human Trafficking Unit (AHTU) network. Rate of 0.3 per lakh is well-controlled.
11. Key benchmarking targets for AP 2025–26Based on comparable population and development profile: (a) Target Telangana (IPC rate 71.2) on overall crime control, (b) Target Tamil Nadu on rape rate (1.1), (c) Target West Bengal and Punjab on SC atrocity prevention (rates 0.4 and 1.3), (d) Target Karnataka on cybercrime detection infrastructure, (e) Target UP and Himachal on acid attack control (rates ~0.3–1.0).

Data: Crime in India 2024 (Volumes I, II & III) — National Crime Records Bureau, Ministry of Home Affairs, Govt. of India.

Crime rates are per lakh population. Rates for SC/ST crimes are per lakh SC/ST population. States/UTs may not be compared purely on the basis of crime figures. Population: Technical Group on Population Projections, July 2020.
